uOttawa Japanese Club

Description

Since I was a freshman, I have been involved in the University of Ottawa Japanese club where we do weekly cultural/learning activities related with Japan and their language. I was also an excutif for one year which motivated me to contribute to the club's future and success. To give more information to the club members as well as to the newcomers, I decided to design and create a website for the club which showcased the basic information of the club. I also added a form which sends a message automatically to the club's email which is mainly used to sign-up for the newsletter.

Another issue that the club encountered during the years was during the club fair where most of the recruiting happens. During that period, the club used to write down the name and email address of the people that were interested in the club on a piece of paper. The issue with this is that once the day is over, someone needs to enter all the emails one by one into the club's email list which can take a long time and errors can happen. To overcome this issue and save a lot of time, I created a registration software which can export the emails into a easily copied format.

Main Features

JELLO's website

  • About: Club's description and current executives names and pictures
  • News: General information and a Facebook iframe with the latest news
  • Register: Send an automatic email to the club asking to be added to the newsletter list
  • Toggle languages from English and French
  • Responsive UI with bootstrap

You can visit the website at jello-aelj.github.io/

Registration software

  • Register a new member (name, email, language level, comments)
  • View all registered members information
  • Export names, emails, language levels and/or comments
